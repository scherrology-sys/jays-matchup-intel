Bottom Line
Wrobleski is a LHP who has made 33 starts across three seasons. His defining characteristic is how differently he pitches to left-handed and right-handed hitters. Against lefties, the Slider and Sinker combine for nearly 67% of his pitches with almost no Changeup. Against righties, the 4-Seam takes over. This is not a pitcher who balances the arsenal by handedness. He leans hard into movement pitches against same-handed hitters.

How the ball moves
As a LHP, the 4-Seam rides up and glove-side. The Sinker runs arm-side into RHH and away from LHH. The Slider sweeps glove-side and stays flat, a classic LHP put-away weapon vs same-handed hitters.
The defining LHP characteristic: Wrobleski throws the Changeup to left-handed hitters 0.3% of the time across his career. Martin did the same thing last night, except that was a deviation from his career. For Wrobleski, this is the career pattern. Left-handed hitters will see Sliders and Sinkers all night with no soft offering to worry about. Whether that is good or bad for the Jays lineup depends entirely on which individual hitters do what with those pitch types.

The edge comes from the right-handed hitters. Springer, Guerrero, and Kirk all face a pitch mix they can handle from the right side. Left-handed hitters are the suppressed group tonight. Varsho, Lukes, and Giménez all project below their baselines. Whether the RHH advantage is enough to offset the LHH difficulty depends on where in the order lefties sit relative to when Wrobleski is most sharp.
What makes the edge real
Wrobleski's Slider is his best pitch by xwOBA (.253) but it runs away from right-handed hitters. Against RHH he goes to the 4-Seam first and mixes the Cutter in heavily. Springer and Guerrero face a more balanced attack than LHH do. The Curveball and Cutter have been hittable across his career. If those are in play, the right side of the lineup can do damage.
What kills the edge
Slider command. When the Slider is locating against lefties, there is no viable alternative pitch to look for and the at-bat is nearly unwinnable. Across his career the Slider xwOBA is .253, his best pitch. If it is sharp tonight, LHH will struggle and the top of the order loses roughly half its projected edge. The velocity drop on the fastball (-1.7 mph in his 2026 debut) is also worth watching.
