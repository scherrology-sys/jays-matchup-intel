Bottom Line
Bradley is a four-pitch pitcher whose weapons play off each other by design. The 4-Seam at 97.0 mph sets up everything. The Curveball at 81.6 creates a 15-mph gap. The Cutter and Splitter fill the middle, both generating whiff rates above 31%. His velocity is trending up in 2026: career 4-Seam average is 96.3, through three starts it is 97.0. That difference matters more than it looks when the off-speed is already working.
Best weapons by miss-bat rate: Curveball (36.8% whiff, .175 xwOBA) · Cutter (32.3% whiff, .298 xwOBA) · Splitter (31.3% whiff, .233 xwOBA). Three legitimate strikeout pitches off the same arm slot. The 4-Seam at 19.6% whiff is the zone-setter, not the finisher.

The opening
Work the count
An 8.3% weighted walk rate is the structural vulnerability. When Bradley falls behind, he has to come into the zone with a fastball that plays differently over the heart of the plate than on the corners. A lineup that takes pitches early in at-bats forces him to throw strikes he does not want to throw. That is the collective blueprint tonight.
