Bottom Line
Soriano has five active pitches in 2026. The Sinker and Knuckle Curve together account for nearly 58% of his mix. The Knuckle Curve at .230 xwOBA and 42.8% whiff rate is the second-best pitch in the file — essentially identical to the Splitter (.218, 37.6%). His K% jumped from a career 22.0% to 32.5% in five 2026 starts on the strength of these two put-away pitches combined with velocity over 97 mph on the Sinker and 4-Seam.
Double blind spot for LHH. The Knuckle Curve is 19.7% of LHH pitches and the Splitter is 19.7%. No current Jay has ≥10 qualifying PA against either pitch. Together they represent 39.4% of the LHH mix and both fall back to each hitter's wOBA_overall. The model cannot price two of Soriano's best weapons for left-handed hitters.
Guerrero barely clears MIN_PA on KC. He has 11 PA vs Knuckle Curves from RHP in the hitter history file (.438 wOBA). That clears the MIN_PA=10 threshold and enters the RHH computation at 31.0% weight. It is a thin sample but it counts.
