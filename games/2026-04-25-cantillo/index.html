Bottom Line
Cantillo is a four-pitch LHP built around a Changeup that ranks among the best in the game. At .199 xwOBA and 48.3% whiff it generates elite contact suppression. The 4-Seam at 91.8 mph is below average velocity for a starter but the combination of the Changeup and Curveball (.218 xwOBA) makes the fastball effective. His K% at 29.1% is consistent with his career 27.3% , no dramatic 2026 change, just a reliable profile.
Changeup: .199 xwOBA · 48.3% whiff. This is the best pitch the Jays have faced this season by xwOBA. The Changeup is deployed heavily vs RHH (24.5% of RHH pitches) and sparingly vs LHH (10.6%). Every right-handed hitter in the lineup will see it regularly. For lefties the primary challenge is the 4-Seam and Slider combination.
LHP note: Slider is the primary LHH weapon at 37.2%. The same-hand matchup sees heavy Slider usage. LHH hitters face a very different pitch diet than RHH. The model accounts for this through the handedness-specific mix, but it means the LHH Edge calls are driven by 4-Seam splits with significant Slider fallback for most hitters.
