What Kay showed us
The Sweeper jumped to 26% usage and was on profile all night. That, combined with the Slider elevated to 21%, gave him five distinct looks against righties. The Changeup held its role as the put-away pitch. This is a pitcher with a deep enough arsenal to be effective even when hitters know what's coming. The analysis will weight the Sweeper considerably higher for the next time we see him.
What the Jays showed us
Springer went 0-for-2. Heineman went 0-for-2. Both were held to flyouts by a pitcher who kept the Changeup away from lefties, exactly as projected. The problem wasn't the read, it was that Kay's Sweeper made the fastball counts much harder to navigate than a 17% usage pitcher should. Guerrero's home run was the one time the lineup punished a mis-located pitch. That's the blueprint for next time.
