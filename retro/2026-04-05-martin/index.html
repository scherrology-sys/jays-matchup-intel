Starting with this game, we are building pitch sequencing into the analysis. Not just what he throws, but what comes next. Tonight showed exactly why it matters.
The Cutter reveals itself as a setup pitch, not a primary one. Across Martin's career, when he throws a Cutter to a left-handed hitter, the Changeup follows 45% of the time. The Cutter is how he sets up the Changeup. Tonight he threw the Cutter at 21% to LHH but the Changeup at only 6%. The sequence broke down. He was using the setup without the payoff pitch.
