Patrick: 7 IP, 2K, 2BB. The Cutter was the story. 34 pitches, 42.9% whiff rate, zero hits. A pitch the model had at .299 xwOBA was essentially untouchable tonight.

What Patrick Did

The Cutter was the game. 34 pitches. Zero hits. 42.9% whiff rate. He threw it to right-handed hitters nearly half the time and they couldn't do a thing with it. Seven fly balls and ground balls and line drives into gloves. The pitch that looks hittable at 89 mph kept cutting away at the last moment.

He also made a clear in-game decision. The Slurve showed up right where the preview expected it, 13% to LHH, replacing the Changeup that he barely threw. First time this lineup saw it. They put some balls in play off it but nobody squared it up.

Velocity sat at 94.6 mph, exactly where it should be. First-pitch strikes 62.5%. Solid but not dominant. He did not need to be dominant. The Cutter did the work.

What the Jays Did

Three hits in seven innings. Guerrero got a single. Two others reached on walks. That was the offense.

The Cutter was specifically the problem for right-handed hitters. Zero hits on 34 Cutter pitches tells you everything. The Jays were making contact, just weak contact. Fly balls. Ground balls. Nothing squared up.

Left-handed hitters saw a more balanced mix and didn't do much with it either. Lukes, Sánchez, and Giménez combined for zero hits in seven plate appearances. Patrick's 4-Seam at 94 with plus movement is a different problem than 99 mph. Timing was off all night.

Interesting Findings
The Cutter paradox
Career xwOBA on the Cutter is .299. That looks like a solid but hittable pitch. Tonight it produced a 42.9% whiff rate and zero hits against this lineup. One of those numbers is right. Probably the whiff rate. The career xwOBA is the average across every lineup he's faced. This lineup may match up particularly poorly against a Cutter that cuts away from right-handed hitters.
Best ranking game of the season
Spearman rho of +0.857. The model had no BVP to work with and still ordered the lineup correctly. Guerrero was the top projection and produced the best result. The bottom projections produced nothing. At LOW confidence, with prior-only estimates, the model still got the order right. That matters for what H2 is actually testing.
Varsho, finally
Walk and two field outs. .232 actual vs .310 projected. Still below expectation, but this is the first game in five where he didn't significantly outperform a suppressed or neutral call. Four straight games of blowing up the model. This one, he didn't. Still monitoring, but the streak may be breaking.
A pattern worth watching
Two LOW confidence first-exposure games in a row, Misiorowski and Patrick. Both times naive beats champion on MAE. Both times the ranking holds up. The model can't calibrate magnitude without BVP. It can still order hitters. If this pattern holds through the season, it becomes a meaningful finding about where the model's value actually lives.
