What Sproat Did

The walk rate was supposed to be the game. Three starts, 20% walk rate, consistent every time. Tonight he walked one hitter in 24 plate appearances. 4.2% walk rate against a lineup that was told to take pitches and make him work counts. He threw first-pitch strikes 62.5% of the time, more than any prior 2026 start. The blueprint was gone before the second inning.

The Sweeper was the weapon. 50% whiff rate on 15 pitches. He threw it to RHH at 31%, up from the expected 23%. It got Guerrero twice. It is a good pitch, the best in his arsenal, and he leaned on it when he needed strikeouts.

The Cutter was flat again. Same pfx_x near zero as the career 2026 trend. One hit allowed on 24 pitches. Still generating outs despite having no horizontal movement.

What the Jays Did

Four hits, one run, seven innings. The only run scored on a Heineman sac bunt in the third inning, not a hit.

Guerrero had the worst game of the night for any projected Edge hitter. 0/3, two strikeouts, a grounded double play. Both strikeouts came on the Sweeper, a pitch the preview flagged as dangerous for RHH. The model flagged it for the wrong hitter but the pitch played out exactly as described.

The bottom of the lineup produced the only positive residuals. Giménez doubled. Clement singled. The hitters the model was least confident about did the most damage. That is a ranking inversion and it hurts the H2 story tonight.

Interesting Findings
The walk rate question
Three games now where the walk rate assumption failed in the same direction, pitchers commanding the zone better than their prior data said they would. Bradley, Misiorowski, Sproat. Worth asking whether this lineup is helping them. If the Jays are swinging at first pitches at an above-average rate in 2026, pitchers are going to look like better zone commanders than they are. That would explain a consistent directional bias across three different pitchers with different profiles.
Jays vs Cutters: still zero hits
48 Cutter pitches in the last two games. Zero hits. Heineman's double tonight broke a brief zero streak but it came on a Cutter with a pfx_x of 0.17, slight cut rather than the flat version. The Jays are 0 for their last two games' worth of Cutters with any meaningful movement. Across all of 2026 in the game files, the Jays have zero hits on real Cutters (pfx_x > 0.20). Something is going on with how this lineup handles the pitch.
Sweeper got the wrong hitter
The preview specifically flagged Leo Jiménez and the Sweeper, 0/2 in BVP with two strikeouts on that pitch. Tonight Sproat threw the Sweeper at Guerrero, and got two strikeouts on pfx_x values of 1.09 and 1.35. The BVP was right about the pitch being dangerous. It just did not help us identify which RHH it would hurt most. Guerrero's 2026 Sweeper exposure is now worth tracking.
Series recap
TOR 1-2 in Milwaukee. Three starters, three different approaches. Misiorowski dominated with a command that did not match his prior data. Patrick was unhittable with a Cutter that had no cut. Sproat eliminated his walk rate for one night. All three times the Jays' offensive blueprint failed because the assumption that defined it did not show up in the game. The common thread is not the pitchers. It might be the lineup.
