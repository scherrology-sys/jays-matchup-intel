What Gallen Did

The Knuckle Curve was the story. The 2026 data going in showed .608 xwOBA and 11.5% whiff rate, a dramatic fall from its career numbers. Tonight he threw it 26 times, more than any other pitch, and it generated a .200 xwOBA with a 26.7% whiff rate. The pitch was back. He used it as both a chase pitch and a put-away pitch and it held up across 25 plate appearances.

The Slider rate to RHH dropped from the expected 44.7% to 31.9%. He used the KC to fill the gap instead. When he did throw the Slider to RHH, hitters made contact, producing 4 singles on 15 pitches. The in-game adjustment to lean on the KC rather than the Slider was the right call given what the KC was doing.

Zero walks in 25 plate appearances. Nine hits. The Changeup generated zero whiffs on 13 pitches. The 4-Seam and Sinker also generated zero whiffs. All three strikeouts came on the Slider or KC. Contact-heavy game, as the model expected.

What the Jays Did

Nine hits, two runs. The two Suppressed calls that outperformed were both right-handed hitters: Clement had a double and a single, Straw had a single. Their hits came on the Knuckle Curve and the Slider respectively. Both were in the bottom of the model's projections entering the game.

Guerrero had one single in three plate appearances, below his Edge projection. Schneider, Varsho, and Barger did not face Gallen. The lineup turned over eight times in six innings and the top of the order did not get a third crack at him.

The KC recovery
Four starts of 2026 data showed .608 xwOBA and 11.5% whiff on the Knuckle Curve. The preview flagged it as an unstable signal. Tonight: 26 pitches, .200 xwOBA, 26.7% whiff. Five balls in play produced four field outs and one line drive single. The career number is .289. The 2026 sample was 57 pitches. Tonight was 26 pitches. The retro memory update is to revert the KC assumption toward the career value for the next Gallen preview. The in-season signal was small and the recovery was real.
Slider to RHH: 4 singles on 15 pitches
Gallen pulled back on the Slider against RHH. It came in at 31.9% instead of 44.7%. When he did use it, hitters made contact: 4 singles, 2 whiffs, 3 fouls on 15 pitches. The xwOBA on those 4 singles ranged from .099 to .501. Mixed quality. Two were genuine hard contact, two were weaker. The in-game adjustment away from the Slider may reflect Gallen reading that it was not working and pivoting to the KC instead.
