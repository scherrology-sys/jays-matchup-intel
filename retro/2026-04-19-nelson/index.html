What Nelson Did

He threw 30 pitches and allowed 8 hits. The pitch mix was correct: 53.3% 4-Seam, 26.7% Slider, 16.7% Curveball, trace Cutter. No Changeup. The Slider to RHH was 33.3%, matching the preview assumption almost exactly. The walk rate was 10%, within the expected range.

The whiff rates on individual pitches were decent: Slider 33.3%, Curveball 33.3%, 4-Seam 27.3%. The pitches were not flat. Hitters were just putting the ball in play and finding holes. Six of the eight hits were 4-Seamers. The fastball was getting hit hard across the entire lineup order.

What the Jays Did

Eight consecutive plate appearances with a hit or walk before the inning ended. Guerrero singled to start it. Clement doubled. Lukes singled. Sanchez singled. Eloy singled. Gimenez singled. Okamoto doubled. Straw walked on a 3-0 fastball. Valenzuela struck out. Lukes doubled again to bat for the second time in the inning.

The lineup went through the order and into the second time around without getting the third out. The eight runs scored were all in the first inning off Nelson. TOR added two more runs off the ARI bullpen later: Okamoto home run in the third, Gimenez sac fly in the fourth.

Interesting Findings
Assumptions held, results did not
The three preview assumptions captured Nelson's profile exactly. Changeup zero. Slider 33.3% to RHH. Walk rate 10%. The model correctly described the pitcher and the lineup still scored 8 runs in one inning. This is the clearest illustration of what assumptions measure: pitch mix and command tendencies, not contact quality. Eight hits in nine balls in play is beyond the scope of any pre-game model.
Clement: Suppressed, led off with a double
The model had Clement at .288 Suppressed. He batted second and doubled on a first-pitch 4-Seam. His career Slider split vs RHP is .243 in 85 PA, which drove the Suppressed call. Nelson never threw him a Slider. The one pitch Clement saw was a 4-Seam, and the model's Suppressed call was based on a different pitch entirely. One PA is not scoreable but it illustrates the limitation of pre-game tier placements when the actual pitch sequence differs.
Lukes batted twice
Lukes is the only hitter with ≥2 PA against Nelson (single and double). exp .325, actual wOBA 1.079, residual +0.754. He is the only scoreable data point and he massively overperformed. That is not model signal — it is a 2-PA sample in an 8-run inning. The double came on a first-pitch Curveball (0-1 count). The single came on a first-pitch 4-Seam.
