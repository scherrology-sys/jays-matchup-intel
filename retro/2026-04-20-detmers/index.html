Jays Matchup Intel retro · 4-20-26. TOR 5, LAA 2.

Detmers: 7 IP, 5K, 2BB. Slider at 42.1% whiff rate. Jays win.

Champion beats naive for the first time this season. 0.301 vs 0.303. Margin is narrow but the direction is right.

Guerrero hit the Changeup twice — single and a 2-run HR. His Changeup split vs LHP was .328 in 35 PA. The model called that matchup.

Varsho actual wOBA .363, exp .363. Exact match.

Heineman (.442 Edge) went 0 for 2 with a K. Clement (.375 Edge) went 0 for 3. The ranking at the top inverted. rho -0.238.

Running n: ~134 hitter-games, 20 observations.

scherrology-sys.github.io/jays-matchup-intel · #BlueJays
What Detmers Did

The Slider was the story. He threw 37 of them at a 42.1% whiff rate. The pitch mix was nearly identical to what the preview expected: Slider at 41.1% overall (expected around 33-37%), 4-Seam at 32.2%, Changeup at 13.3%, Curveball at 12.2%. The Changeup and K rate assumptions both held.

Three Slider strikeouts: Varsho on 0-2, Sosa on 0-2, Schneider on 3-2. Heineman struck out on the Curveball at 2-2. Okamoto punched out on a 3-2 4-Seam. Five strikeouts, three different pitches. The Slider was generating whiffs even when it was not ending at-bats.

Guerrero hit him for two Changeups. Both were early-count pitches. The 1-0 Changeup became a two-run home run. The model's Changeup assumption held exactly and Guerrero's Changeup split was the right number to have in the computation.

What the Jays Did

Five hits and a win. Guerrero drove in two with the home run in the third inning. Eloy had two singles. Varsho took a hit by pitch. The Jays scored three times off Detmers and added two more off the bullpen later.

Heineman and Clement both went hitless. Heineman struck out and grounded into a double play in two PA. Clement had three field outs in three PA. Both were Edge projections. Neither validated.

Interesting Findings
Varsho: exact projection
exp .363, actual .363. Field out, strikeout on Slider, hit by pitch. The HBP wOBA weight is 0.726, denominator is 2 (excluding HBP), giving 0.726/2 = .363 exactly. This is a coincidence of arithmetic, not a model result, but it is the cleanest number in the season log. The projection was built from 4-Seam and Sinker splits with Slider, Curveball, and Changeup falling back to prior. The prior of .318 vs LHP anchored the computation and it landed on the right number.
Guerrero and the Changeup
Two Changeup hits: single on 2-1, home run on 1-0. His Changeup split vs LHP was .328 in 35 PA, which entered the computation at 13.9% of the RHH mix. That specific pitch-type split doing exactly what the model priced is the clearest validation of the computation logic this season. The model knew he handles the Changeup. Detmers threw it at the expected rate. Guerrero hit it.
Heineman Edge miss
The highest projection in the file at .442. Strikeout on a Curveball (2-2) and a groundout. His Edge call was built on a strong prior vs LHP (.398) with 4-Seam as the only pitch that entered the computation — Slider, Changeup, and Curveball all fell back to prior. When a projection is driven primarily by a high prior with limited pitch-split signal, it carries more variance. Heineman's .398 prior vs LHP is real but the lack of Slider split data means the model could not confirm how he handles Detmers' primary weapon.
