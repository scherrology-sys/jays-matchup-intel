What Cantillo Did

Six innings, six hits, two runs, one walk, four strikeouts. The arsenal was the same four pitches but the deployment was completely different from his 2026 average. Against RHH he used the Changeup at 42.9%, nearly double the 24.5% expected. Against LHH the Curveball dominated at 47.4%, with the Slider barely appearing at 10.5%.

The result was effective: two runs in six innings. The elevated Changeup rate vs RHH generated whiffs but also contact. The Curveball-heavy LHH approach limited the LHH hitters who were priced against a Slider-heavy diet. Cantillo adapted his plan and it worked.

Interesting Findings
Heineman: nearest Edge match of the season
exp .445, actual .444, residual -0.001. He struck out and singled. His Edge call was built on his 4-Seam split (.493/13PA) vs LHP with Changeup, Curveball, and Slider all falling back to his prior (.398). The prior-driven Edge held. The 4-Seam at 49.8% of RHH pitches was the primary pitch he faced and his split on it is strong. This is the computation doing exactly what it is supposed to do when the primary pitch dominates.
Gimenez Suppressed: held for the second consecutive game

Okamoto: third league-fallback home run in four games
Single and home run in 3 PA (.996 actual vs .320 expected). Okamoto has now hit home runs in back-to-back games, both as a league-fallback Neutral call. With no 2024-25 history in the file, the model has no split data for him. The .320 prior has a wide confidence interval. Three consecutive games where league-fallback hitters have produced significantly above the prior (Okamoto and Sanchez last game, Okamoto again tonight). The pattern is notable as sample builds.
Cantillo's pitch swap
The Changeup and Slider essentially swapped usage vs their respective handedness targets. Changeup nearly doubled vs RHH. Slider nearly disappeared vs LHH, replaced by the Curveball. This is a genuine game-plan adjustment, not noise. For the next Cantillo preview, the assumption tolerances should be wider and both assumptions should be flagged as having high variance relative to the single-game expectations.
