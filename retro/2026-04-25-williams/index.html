What Williams Did

Six innings, seven hits, six runs, two walks, four strikeouts. The arsenal was the same five pitches but the Sweeper ran below its 2026 RHH average at 32.1%. The 4-Seam filled some of that space at 28.3% vs RHH. The Curveball was the most-used pitch vs LHH at 30.2%. The result was a contact-heavy outing: 15 field outs in 27 PA, but seven hits and six runs mixed in.

The K rate at 14.8% is the lowest of his 2026 season so far. The connection to the Sweeper usage is direct. With the put-away pitch running 10 points below its 2026 RHH average, the whiff totals dropped accordingly. Williams got contact outs but gave up hard contact when hitters connected.

Interesting Findings
Two home runs from league-fallback hitters
Okamoto and Sanchez each have no 2024-25 history in the hitter file against RHP pitches. Both were priced at LEAGUE_WOBA=0.320 Neutral. Both hit home runs. The model cannot capture variance on hitters it has no split data for. A .320 prior is the best available estimate with zero history, but it carries a very wide confidence interval. These are the PA where the model is effectively guessing.
Schneider Edge call: near-exact match
Schneider was the top Edge projection at .365. He walked and struck out for a .348 actual wOBA, a residual of -0.017. That is the closest any Edge call has come to its projection this season. His 4-Seam split (.538/34PA) was the primary driver and the 4-Seam was his most-seen pitch tonight at roughly a third of his PA. The split-based computation worked exactly as designed for this matchup.
Varsho Sweeper signal: directionally held
Varsho's .070 wOBA in 10 qualifying PA vs Sweepers was flagged in the preview as a thin but real signal that pulled her from .360 to .326 Neutral. She went 0-for-3 tonight. The Sweeper ran at 32.6% vs LHH, near the expected 17.4%... wait, it was actually elevated vs LHH, running higher than expected on that side while lower than expected vs RHH. Regardless, Varsho's zero is consistent with the direction the model called, even if the sample behind it was thin.
Sweeper vs RHH vs LHH contrast
The Sweeper ran 32.1% vs RHH (below 42.0% expected) but 32.6% vs LHH (well above 17.4% expected). Williams effectively swapped the intended platoon usage tonight, deploying the Sweeper more symmetrically across both sides rather than using it as a primary RHH weapon. This flipped the assumption on both ends simultaneously. The net effect: RHH saw less Sweeper than the model priced, LHH saw more.
